Plants and Gardening

Quick Gardening Tips

  • Water your plants at the base near their roots and not on their leaves
  • You can use coffee grounds as natural fertilizer
  • Rotate potted plants for even sunlight and growth

I have always loved plants and gardening. They are my favorite hobbies and interests because they can help with relaxation and connecting with nature.

Whether its growing herbs, caring for houseplants, or planting flowers, I love taking care of them and watching them grow.

Types of Plants

Aquatic Plants

Aquatic plants have adapted to living in aquatic environments, they are able to be a source of food and shelter for different types of aquatic organisms.

They can be found in habitats including rivers, lakes, ponds, wetlands, and more.

The three main types include emergent, floating, and submerged.

Some examples of aquatic plants include waterlily's, lotus, duckweeds, and algae.

Houseplants

Houseplants are one of the most popular plants in homes due to their aesthetic appeal and health benefits.

Each species of house plant has its own requirements for light, water, and care, so it is important to choose plants that best fit your lifestyle and home environment.

Some of the easiest houseplants to take care of include spider plants, pothos, succulents, and monstera.

Roses

Roses are one of the most beautiful, fragrant, and diverse flowers. They are classified into three main groups: species roses, old garden roses, and modern garden roses.

Species roses are wild roses which attract pollinators. Old garden roses are characterized by their strong fragrance and resilience like Damasks, Albas, and Bourbons.

Modern garden roses were developed after the 1860s and include Floribundas, Grandifloras, and Hybrid Teas. They are most known for their impressive color range, repetitive blooming, and varied growth habits.
